# Marine Environmental Research submission requirements

Accessed: 2026-07-22

Official source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/marine-environmental-research/publish/guide-for-authors

## Article and review model

- Target article type: Full-length Article (permitted).
- Typical length: 5,000–10,000 words; no more than 50 references.
- Review: single anonymized, normally with at least two reviewers. Author identities therefore remain in the submission manuscript; a blinded companion is optional and not an official requirement.
- Title: concise and informative; no numerical character limit is stated. Avoid abbreviations and formulae where possible.
- Abstract: concise, factual, standalone, unstructured by journal rule, and no more than 250 words. References and uncommon abbreviations should be avoided.
- Keywords: 1–7, in English; avoid multiword terms and non-established abbreviations where possible.
- Main structure: clearly defined, consecutively numbered sections and subsections; the abstract is unnumbered. Acknowledgments appear in a separate section directly before the references.
- Full-length article content: detailed methods, analyses, comprehensive results, and meaningful conclusions presenting original research within scope.

## Files and formatting

- Editable source files are required. Word manuscripts must be `.doc` or `.docx`, single column; PDF is not an acceptable source file.
- No journal-specific font, spacing, page-numbering, or line-numbering mandate is stated in the current guide. The package supplies continuous line numbering and page numbering for review convenience.
- Title-page information: concise title; author names in submission order; affiliations with full postal addresses and country; corresponding-author designation and current contact details; present address only if applicable.
- Tables: editable text, consecutively numbered and cited; captions supplied; notes below the body; avoid vertical rules and cell shading. No numerical table limit is stated; use tables sparingly and avoid duplication.
- Figures: cite and number in sequence; submit each as a separate logically named file with a caption. No numerical figure limit is stated.
- Vector artwork: EPS or PDF with fonts embedded or converted to graphics.
- Halftones: TIFF, JPG, or PNG at ≥300 dpi (≥1063 px single-column, ≥2244 px full-page width).
- Line art: TIFF, JPG, or PNG at ≥1000 dpi (≥3543 px single-column, ≥7480 px full-page width).
- Combination art: TIFF, JPG, or PNG at ≥500 dpi (≥1772 px single-column, ≥3740 px full-page width).
- Colour figures must remain understandable to readers with colour-vision impairments.
- Supplementary material: submit with the manuscript; every file must be cited and have a concise descriptive caption. Files can ordinarily be added or replaced only at revision after initial submission.

## Required and optional submission materials

- Highlights: required as a separate editable file; 3–5 bullets; ≤85 characters including spaces per bullet.
- Graphical abstract: encouraged, not required. If supplied, 531 × 1328 px (height × width) or proportionally larger, readable at 5 × 13 cm; preferred TIFF, EPS, PDF, or MS Office. Not included because it is optional.
- Cover letter: no journal-specific mandatory content or explicit requirement is stated for Full-length Articles; a tailored letter is included.
- Reporting checklist: no study-design-specific checklist is mandated. SAGER guidance is recommended where sex/gender is relevant; it is not applicable to these checklist-level outcomes.
- Corresponding-author checklist information includes full contact details, including full postal address and telephone number.

## References, data, and declarations

- In-text citations: numbers in square brackets, numbered in order of first appearance.
- Reference list: numbered in citation order; journal names abbreviated according to LTWA; DOI links recommended. Formatting may be consistent at submission, but the guide supplies the numbered journal style.
- Research data: Option C. Deposit data in a relevant repository and cite/link them, or explain why sharing is not possible. A data-availability statement is required at submission.
- Author contributions: CRediT roles are required for co-author contribution reporting; applicable roles are supplied for the sole author.
- Funding: funding sources and sponsor roles must be disclosed; the guide supplies a standard no-funding sentence.
- Competing interests: the Elsevier declarations tool must be completed and its `.doc`/`.docx` output uploaded; signatures are not required.
- Ethics/permits: no journal-specific ecological permit form is stated. Applicability must be declared accurately.
- Generative AI: material use during manuscript preparation must be declared before the references after human review and editing. AI tools cannot be authors.
- Preprints: permitted under Elsevier's sharing policy and do not count as prior publication. Optional SSRN posting is offered during submission.

## Submission checklist implications

- Designate one corresponding author and enter all author details consistently in the manuscript and submission system.
- Upload keywords, captions, editable tables, separate figures, supplement, and highlights.
- Confirm spelling/grammar, reference reciprocity, and permissions for third-party material.
- Open-access charges apply only if that route is selected; no selection is made in this package.

## Unresolved author-supplied fields

- Full postal address for the University of Victoria affiliation/corresponding author.
- Corresponding-author telephone number.
- Human confirmation of the proposed generative-AI declaration after final review.
- Human confirmation of originality, exclusive submission, approval to submit, and any prior-preprint status in the cover letter/submission system.

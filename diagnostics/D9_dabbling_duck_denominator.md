# D9 — Dabbling-duck denominator

The support-qualified species universe is the 58-row
`metadata/canonical_species_registry.csv`. Within it there are exactly **five
dabbling ducks**:

| Species | Guild id | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Mallard | surface_vegetation_roe | core (modeled) |
| American Wigeon | surface_vegetation_roe | core (modeled) |
| Northern Pintail | surface_vegetation_roe | core (modeled) |
| Gadwall | falsification | specificity comparator |
| Northern Shoveler | falsification | specificity comparator |

The manuscript's "four of five dabbling ducks" refers to these five
support-qualified taxa: four (Mallard count, Northern Pintail detection, American
Wigeon both, Northern Shoveler detection) show a positive active interaction;
Gadwall does not. The statement is correct for the modeled set.

Two clarifications for Phase 3 item 14:

1. The denominator is the five **support-qualified** dabbling ducks, not the
   regional dabbling-duck community. Other North American dabblers (teal, Wood
   Duck, Eurasian Wigeon) occur in the Strait of Georgia but did not meet the
   support criteria and are absent from the modeled family and from the protected
   state file, so they cannot be counted here. Wording should say "the five
   dabbling ducks that met support criteria".
2. The three core dabblers are assigned to a `surface_vegetation_roe` guild, that
   is, a hypothesised weak surface- and vegetation-associated roe-use pathway.
   They are not mechanism-free. The v7 Discussion phrase "no established
   roe-feeding pathway" overstates this. Routed to `OPEN_QUESTIONS.md` for the
   Phase 4 Discussion 4.3 edit.
